Modeling the Isotope Composition of Groundwater Using Hydrochemical Properties in Eastern Saudi Arabia: Implementation of Innovative Data Intelligence Techniques

Abstract

Study region: Al-Qatif coastal region in eastern Saudi Arabia is an arid area with limited surface water resources and is highly vulnerable to seawater intrusion.

Study focus: This research focuses on modeling and predicting the isotope composition (δ¹⁸O and δ²H) of coastal groundwater using Artificial Intelligence (AI) models. It leverages a readily available hydrochemical groundwater dataset to understand the geochemical evolution and the impact of seawater intrusion in arid coastal environments.

New hydrological insight for the region: Eight AI algorithms—K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Support Vector Regression (SVR), Random Forest (RF), Extra Trees (ET), Bagging, AdaBoost (AdaBt), Gradient Boosting (GRB), and CatBoost (CAT)—along with stacking ensemble models, were developed to predict δ¹⁸O and δ²H isotopes. The dataset includes physicochemical parameters, ions, elements, and isotopic measurements from 47 wells.

The stacking ensemble models outperformed individual models. The best model for δ¹⁸O (O_M1) achieved an R² of 0.9858, MAE of 0.0440, and Pearson correlation of 0.9941. For δ²H, the optimal model (H_M1) achieved an R² of 0.9317, MAE of 0.5334, and Pearson correlation of 0.9658.

These findings reveal that the variation in groundwater chemistry is primarily associated with mixing processes, largely driven by seawater intrusion. The study highlights the potential of AI-based models to effectively predict isotopic signatures and understand groundwater dynamics in coastal arid regions.

Highlights

  1. Predictive models of δ18O and δ2H isotopes of coastal groundwater were developed.
  2. Groundwater's physicochemical properties and major ions utilized.
  3. The stacking ensemble models outperform traditional machine learning techniques.
  4. The best models achieve an accuracy of 0.98 for δ18O and 0.93 for δ2H.
  5. Calcium, chloride, and bromide are the main influences.
